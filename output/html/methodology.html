Calculating Opportunity

  • Vacancy = Unleased space (empty suites). Only applies to multi-tenant buildings.
  • Utilization = People present in occupied space. A fully-leased office with 50% WFH has 0% vacancy but 50% utilization.
Building CategoryOpportunity Formula
Multi-tenant (Office, Medical Office, Mixed Use) Vacancy + (1 - Vacancy) × (1 - Utilization)
Single-operator (Hotel, School, Retail, Venue) 1 - Utilization

Modifiers

Automation (0-1): 50% year-built + 25% building size + 25% zip income tier.[1]

Efficiency (0.85×-1.10×): Based on ENERGY STAR score. Efficient buildings have less waste; inefficient buildings have more.[2]

Climate: 1.10× Northern, 1.05× North-Central, 1.00× South-Central, 0.95× Southern.

Master Formula

ODCV% = (Floor + Opportunity × Automation × Range) × EfficiencyMod × ClimateMod

Where Range = Ceiling - Floor for each building type.

Converting to Dollars

Buildings report energy consumption. We convert to cost using utility rates by zip code:

  • Electricity: Energy charges ($/kWh × 1.10× for taxes/fees) + Demand charges ($/kW × 1.265× for ratchets/penalties)
  • Natural Gas: kBtu ÷ 100 = therms × rate × 1.10×
  • District Steam: kBtu ÷ 909 = Mlb × rate
  • Fuel Oil: kBtu ÷ 1000 = MMBtu × rate × 1.10×

Peak demand estimated using building-type load factors (0.30-0.70).[3]

HVAC Share

Not all energy goes to HVAC. Base values by building type and climate zone.[4]

Annual Savings

HVAC_cost = (Elec × hvac_pct_elec) + (Gas × hvac_pct_gas) + (Steam × hvac_pct_steam) + (Fuel_Oil × hvac_pct_oil)
Annual_savings = HVAC_cost × ODCV%

Example: $500K total energy, 45% HVAC ($225K), 30% ODCV = $67,500/year.

ENERGY STAR Impact

ENERGY STAR scores compare a building's energy use to similar buildings nationwide. A score of 50 means median; 75+ qualifies for certification. Lower EUI → higher score. A 10% EUI reduction typically improves score by 5-15 points.
