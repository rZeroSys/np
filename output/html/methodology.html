How Savings Are Calculated

Nationwide Prospector calculates potential ODCV savings through this pipeline:

  1. Estimate HVAC share of energy — Using CBECS data to determine what % of each fuel goes to HVAC
  2. Compute ODCV savings % — Based on vacancy, utilization, building type, and efficiency modifiers
  3. Calculate annual HVAC cost savings — HVAC costs × ODCV savings %
  4. Add BPS fine avoidance — Reduced emissions can lower regulatory fines in cities with Building Performance Standards
  5. Compute valuation uplift — Total OpEx avoided ÷ cap rate = increase in building value

ODCV Savings Model

What the ODCV % Represents

odcv_hvac_savings_pct is the fraction of HVAC costs saved by ODCV, applied to HVAC-only costs, and bounded by building-type ranges.

The Master Formula

The model uses a floor + scaled opportunity structure:

ODCV_base = Floor + (Opportunity × Automation × (Ceiling - Floor))

Then applies multiplicative modifiers:

ODCV_final = ODCV_base × EfficiencyModifier × ClimateModifier

...and clamps to building-type bounds.

Opportunity Score (Vacancy + Utilization)

Vacancy data from CBRE; utilization from Kastle Back to Work Barometer.

Multi-tenant Commercial (office, medical office, mixed use)

Opportunity = V + (1-V) × (1-U)

where V = vacancy rate, U = utilization rate

Single-operator Buildings (schools, hotels, retail)

Opportunity = 1 - U

Limited-opportunity (hospitals, labs)

Opportunity = (1-U) × 0.3

Data Centers

Opportunity = 0

Automation Score

Automation = (YearScore + SizeScore) / 2

Size-score bands: under 50k sqft → 0.25; over 250k sqft → 1.0

Applying ODCV to Energy

ODCV savings apply to HVAC portion of each fuel:

Electricity HVAC Savings = Electricity Use × %Electric HVAC × ODCV%
Gas HVAC Savings = Gas Use × %Gas HVAC × ODCV%
Steam HVAC Savings = Steam Use × %Steam HVAC × ODCV%

Annual HVAC Utility Savings (USD)

odcv_hvac_savings_annual_usd = hvac_cost_total_annual × odcv_hvac_savings_pct

Carbon Reduction

Using EPA eGRID factors:

elec_reduction_kbtu = elec_kbtu × hvac_pct_elec × odcv_pct
gas_reduction_kbtu = gas_kbtu × hvac_pct_gas × odcv_pct
steam_reduction_kbtu = steam_kbtu × hvac_pct_steam × odcv_pct

Year-1 carbon reduction stored as: odcv_carbon_reduction_yr1_mt

Total Annual OpEx Avoidance

Total Annual OpEx Avoidance is the sum of:

  • Annual HVAC utility savings
  • Year-1 BPS fine avoidance
total_annual_opex_avoidance = odcv_hvac_savings_annual_usd + bps_fine_avoided_yr1_usd

Note: Valuation increase is a downstream capitalization of OpEx avoided; it is not itself an annual operating expense.

Valuation Impact

Core Concept

If a building avoids $1 in annual operating expense, NOI increases by $1; value increase is NOI uplift divided by cap rate. See Direct Capitalization (I ÷ R = V).

Valuation Uplift Formula

val_odcv_impact_usd = savings_opex_avoided_annual_usd / (cap_rate / 100)

Estimating Current Value

When true valuation is unknown:

Estimated Gross Income = Total Energy Cost / 0.12
Estimated NOI = Estimated Gross Income × 0.60
val_current_usd = Estimated NOI / (cap_rate / 100)

Then:

val_post_odcv_usd = val_current_usd + val_odcv_impact_usd

BPS Fine Methodology

Nationwide Prospector computes: baseline year-1 fine (no ODCV), post-ODCV year-1 fine, and year-1 avoided fine. See IMT BPS Matrix for cross-city comparison.

bps_fine_avoided_yr1 = fine_baseline - fine_postODCV

Emissions-Cap Laws

NYC LL97 (2024-2029)

Cap = sqft × coefficient
Overage = max(0, emissions - cap)
Fine = overage × $268/tCO2e

Boston BERDO 2.0

Penalty rate = $234/tCO2e

Cambridge BEUDO

Penalty rate = $234/tCO2e

DC BEPS (ENERGY STAR Score Target)

Target score = 71
Fine = sqft × $10 × (gap / target_score)
Cap = $7.5M
Note

Buildings without ENERGY STAR score cannot be fined under this pathway and are excluded from the DC fine calculation.

Energize Denver (EUI Target)

Target EUI (office): 48.3
Overage kBtu = max(0, (EUI - target) × sqft)
Fine = overage_kBtu × $0.30

Seattle BEPS (Emissions Intensity)

Target GHGI = 0.0023 tCO2e/sqft (commercial)
Fine = (emissions over cap) × penalty_rate

St. Louis BEPS

If EUI > 72: Fine = $500/day × 15 days = $7,500

San Francisco EBEPO

Reporting-only (no fine avoidance calculated).

Estimated Post-ODCV ENERGY STAR Score

What an ENERGY STAR Score Is

ENERGY STAR is based on an efficiency ratio:

Efficiency Ratio = Actual Source EUI / Predicted Source EUI

Lower ratio → higher score; mapping uses a gamma distribution per building type.

How Prospector Estimates Post-ODCV Score

  1. Weighted HVAC fraction: hvac_total_pct = Σ(hvac_pct × energy) / total_energy
  2. Total energy reduction: total_energy_reduction = odcv_pct × hvac_total_pct
  3. New EUI: new_eui = current_eui × (1 - total_energy_reduction)
  4. Convert score ↔ ratio using gamma inverse CDF/CDF with building-type parameters

Technical Background

Utility Cost Calculation

Convert annual energy consumption + rate assumptions into annual utility costs.

Electricity (with Demand)

Peak demand estimated via load factor (default: 0.45):

peak_kw = energy_elec_kwh / (8760 × load_factor)

cost_elec_energy = energy_elec_kwh × rate_kwh × 1.10
cost_elec_demand = peak_kw × rate_demand_kw × 12 × 1.265

Multipliers: 1.10 for delivery/taxes; 1.265 for demand ratchets.

Natural Gas

cost_gas_annual = (energy_gas_kbtu / 100) × rate_therm × 1.10

District Steam

cost_steam_annual = (energy_steam_kbtu / 909) × rate_mlb

Fuel Oil

cost_fuel_oil_annual = (energy_fuel_oil_kbtu / 1000) × rate_mmbtu × 1.10

HVAC Percentage by Fuel Type

ODCV savings apply to HVAC only. HVAC shares are grounded in CBECS 2018 microdata with adjustments for:

  • ENERGY STAR score thresholds
  • Year-built (older buildings often have less optimized systems)
  • EUI vs peer median (combined adjustment cap ±12%)

Electric HVAC has a minimum floor of 15% for fans, pumps, and controls.

Special Cases

  • Hotels: gas mostly DHW + cooking; HVAC is smaller fraction
  • Restaurants: gas dominated by cooking; HVAC fraction dampened
  • Data centers: assigned distinct HVAC shares (no direct CBECS category)
