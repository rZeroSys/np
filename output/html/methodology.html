1) What Nationwide Prospector Produces

1.1 Output Artifact

The core output is a building-level table (portfolio_data.csv) with one row per building and a standardized set of column groups (location, building characteristics, energy, costs, HVAC %, occupancy, ODCV savings, carbon, BPS fines, combined savings, valuation, metadata).

1.2 Intended Use

Nationwide Prospector is designed for portfolio-scale screening:

  • Identify buildings likely wasting HVAC energy due to vacancy + under-utilization
  • Quantify annual HVAC utility savings from occupancy-driven ventilation controls (ODCV)
  • Quantify year-1 regulatory penalty avoidance (where Building Performance Standards apply)
  • Translate annual OpEx avoided into property valuation uplift using cap rates
  • Roll buildings up into portfolio organizations for account-based targeting
Important

This is explicitly a screening model, not a replacement for an engineering audit.

2) Data Sources

Nationwide Prospector combines measured/declared building energy with modeled enrichment layers (rates, HVAC end-use splits, occupancy proxies, emissions factors, BPS rule parameters, cap rates).

2.1 Building Energy & Benchmarking Disclosure Data

Primary source class

  • EPA ENERGY STAR Portfolio Manager and/or local benchmarking disclosure laws (city/municipal public disclosure datasets)

Key fields derived

  • Electricity consumption (energy_elec_kwh)
  • Fuel consumption (energy_gas_kbtu, energy_steam_kbtu, energy_fuel_oil_kbtu)
  • Site EUI (energy_site_eui) and building type median benchmark (energy_eui_benchmark)
  • ENERGY STAR score (energy_star_score) when available

2.2 Utility Rates

Energy cost estimation uses:

  • EIA commercial electricity data + local tariff analysis
  • EIA natural gas data + local tariff analysis
  • ConEd steam schedules / utility filings
  • EIA heating oil data / regional price surveys
  • Multipliers calibrated from sample commercial bills and research

2.3 HVAC End-Use Split

HVAC attribution depends on CBECS 2018 microdata (Commercial Buildings Energy Consumption Survey) with additional adjustments and special-case handling.

Output includes: hvac_pct_elec, hvac_pct_gas, hvac_pct_steam, hvac_pct_fuel_oil

2.4 Occupancy Proxies

ODCV needs an estimate of how "empty" the building is:

  • Office vacancy: market vacancy estimates (CBRE / Cushman & Wakefield)
  • Office utilization: badge-swipe utilization proxies (Kastle Systems "Back to Work")
  • Hotel occupancy: market occupancy (STR / CoStar)
  • School calendar utilization: instructional days / operating schedules

2.5 Carbon Emission Factors

Carbon is calculated from raw energy by fuel using:

  • City-specific electricity factors (grid region / BPS-specific definitions)
  • Standard combustion factors for gas
  • Steam factors (varies by system)
  • A national default when city-specific factor is not defined

2.6 Building Performance Standards (BPS) Rules

The national BPS calculator uses city program parameters for:

  • NYC LL97
  • Boston BERDO 2.0
  • Cambridge BEUDO
  • Washington DC BEPS
  • Denver Energize
  • Seattle BEPS
  • St. Louis BEPS
  • San Francisco EBEPO (reporting-only: no fine modeled)

2.7 Cap Rates & Valuation Assumptions

  • Cap rate medians by building type
  • An income capitalization approach where annual OpEx avoided increases NOI dollar-for-dollar

4) Energy Normalization & Unit Conventions

4.1 Core Units

Unit Description
kWhElectricity consumption
kBtuThermal energy unit used for aggregating across fuels
thermGas billing unit (1 therm = 100 kBtu)
Mlb steam"Thousand pounds of steam"
MMBtu1,000 kBtu

4.2 EUI Definition

Site EUI is energy per floor area:

Site EUI = Total site energy (kBtu) / Gross floor area (sqft)

Total site energy is computed from disclosed fuel quantities:

Total kBtu = (kWh × 3.412) + (therms × 100) + (steam MMBtu × 1000) + (fuel oil gallons × 138.5)

5) Utility Cost Calculation Module

5.1 Purpose

Convert annual energy consumption + rate assumptions into annual utility costs for electricity (energy + demand), natural gas, district steam, and fuel oil.

5.2 Electricity: Demand Estimation via Load Factor

If peak demand is not directly known, it is estimated using load factor:

Load factor = Average demand / Peak demand

Peak kW = Annual kWh / (8760 × Load factor)

Typical ranges by building type and a default assumption of 0.45 are used.

5.3 Electricity Annual Cost Formula

peak_kw = energy_elec_kwh / (8760 × load_factor)

cost_elec_energy_annual = energy_elec_kwh × rate_kwh × 1.10

cost_elec_demand_annual = peak_kw × rate_demand_kw × 12 × 1.265

cost_elec_total_annual = cost_elec_energy_annual + cost_elec_demand_annual

The Multipliers

  • 1.10 is an "all-in adder" to approximate delivery charges, taxes/surcharges, and fixed customer charges when the rate field is commodity-only.
  • 1.265 adjusts demand charges for ratchets, seasonal demand premiums, and other demand-related bill structures.

5.4 Natural Gas Annual Cost

therms = energy_gas_kbtu / 100

cost_gas_annual = therms × rate_therm × 1.10

5.5 District Steam Annual Cost

Steam does not apply the 1.10 adder (steam rates are treated as all-inclusive):

mlb = energy_steam_kbtu / 909

cost_steam_annual = mlb × rate_mlb

5.6 Fuel Oil Annual Cost

mmbtu = energy_fuel_oil_kbtu / 1000

cost_fuel_oil_annual = mmbtu × rate_mmbtu × 1.10
Validation

Cost formulas were validated against 23,882 buildings with known costs; match rates are essentially exact.

6) HVAC Percentage by Fuel Type (End-Use Attribution)

6.1 Purpose

ODCV savings apply to HVAC only, so we must estimate how much of each fuel is HVAC-driven:

  • hvac_pct_elec: fraction of electricity attributable to HVAC
  • hvac_pct_gas: fraction of gas attributable to HVAC
  • hvac_pct_steam: fraction of steam attributable to HVAC
  • hvac_pct_fuel_oil: fraction of fuel oil attributable to HVAC

6.2 Data Backbone

The method is grounded in CBECS 2018 microdata with building portfolio join keys and additional refinements.

6.3 Core Adjustment Logic

HVAC percentages are not taken as a single static number. Adjustments incorporate:

  • ENERGY STAR score thresholds (absolute bands) that shift expected HVAC share
  • Year-built adjustments (older buildings often have less optimized systems)
  • EUI vs peer median adjustments (EUI ratio bands)
  • A combined adjustment cap (±12%)

6.4 Electricity HVAC Minimum Floor

Electric HVAC share has a documented minimum of 15% to account for fans, pumps, and controls that run broadly across operating hours.

6.5 Special Cases

Some building types have fundamentally different end-use splits:

  • Hotels: gas is mostly DHW + cooking; HVAC is a smaller fraction with intensity-tier logic
  • Restaurants: gas dominated by cooking; HVAC fraction fixed and dampened
  • Data centers: treated as special (no direct CBECS category) and assigned distinct HVAC shares

6.6 HVAC Totals (Energy & Cost)

Once fuel HVAC fractions exist:

hvac_energy_total_kbtu = Σ(fuel_kbtu × hvac_pct_fuel)

hvac_cost_total_annual = Σ(fuel_cost × hvac_pct_fuel)

7) ODCV Savings Model (the Prospector "Engine")

ODCV = Occupancy-Driven Control Ventilation (ventilate/condition based on real occupancy rather than fixed schedules).

7.1 What the ODCV % Represents

odcv_hvac_savings_pct is the fraction of HVAC costs saved by ODCV, applied to HVAC-only costs, and bounded by building-type ranges.

7.2 The Master Formula

The model uses a floor + scaled opportunity structure:

ODCV_base = Floor + (Opportunity × Automation × (Ceiling - Floor))

Then applies multiplicative modifiers:

ODCV_final = ODCV_base × EfficiencyModifier × ClimateModifier

...and clamps to building-type bounds.

7.3 Opportunity Score (Vacancy + Utilization Logic)

This is explicitly building-type dependent:

A) Multi-tenant Commercial (office, medical office, mixed use, strip mall)

Opportunity captures both space that is vacant but still ventilated, plus leased space that is under-utilized:

Opportunity = V + (1-V) × (1-U)

where V = vacancy rate, U = utilization rate

B) Single-operator Buildings (schools, hotels, retail)

No "vacancy" concept; it's purely utilization-driven:

Opportunity = 1 - U

C) Limited-opportunity (hospitals, labs)

Opportunity is discounted with a multiplier:

Opportunity = (1-U) × 0.3

D) Data Centers

Opportunity = 0

7.4 Automation Score

Automation is modeled as an average of a year-built score and a size score:

Automation = (YearScore + SizeScore) / 2

Size-score bands: under 50k sqft → 0.25; over 250k sqft → 1.0

7.8 Worked Example: ODCV % Computation (Office)

Example: Chicago Office
  • Vacancy = 28%
  • Utilization = 52%
  • Opportunity = 0.626
  • Automation = 0.875
  • Floor/Ceiling = 20% / 40%
  • Climate modifier (North-Central) = 1.05

Final ODCV ≈ 32.6%

8) Applying ODCV to Dollars, Energy, Carbon, BPS, and Valuation

This is where "Prospector" becomes a business case.

8.1 Apply ODCV to HVAC Energy by Fuel

ODCV savings apply to HVAC portion of each fuel:

Electricity HVAC Savings = Electricity Use × %Electric HVAC × ODCV%
Gas HVAC Savings = Gas Use × %Gas HVAC × ODCV%
Steam HVAC Savings = Steam Use × %Steam HVAC × ODCV%
Fuel Oil HVAC Savings = Fuel Oil Use × %Fuel Oil HVAC × ODCV%

8.2 Annual HVAC Utility Savings (USD)

Primary dollar output:

odcv_hvac_savings_annual_usd = hvac_cost_total_annual × odcv_hvac_savings_pct

8.3 Carbon Emissions (Baseline and Post-ODCV)

Baseline Emissions

E_base = kBtu_elec × f_elec + kBtu_gas × f_gas + kBtu_steam × f_steam

Where f = emission factor (tCO2e per kBtu), which varies by city/grid for electricity.

Apply ODCV Reductions per Fuel

elec_reduction_kbtu = elec_kbtu × hvac_pct_elec × odcv_pct
gas_reduction_kbtu = gas_kbtu × hvac_pct_gas × odcv_pct
steam_reduction_kbtu = steam_kbtu × hvac_pct_steam × odcv_pct

Year-1 carbon reduction stored as: odcv_carbon_reduction_yr1_mt

9) Building Performance Standards (BPS) Fine Methodology

Nationwide Prospector computes: baseline year-1 fine (no ODCV), post-ODCV year-1 fine, and year-1 avoided fine (difference).

9.1 General "Fine Avoided" Definition

bps_fine_avoided_yr1 = fine_baseline - fine_postODCV

9.2 Emissions-Cap Laws (NYC LL97, Boston BERDO, Cambridge BEUDO)

NYC LL97 (2024-2029 period)

Cap = sqft × coefficient
Overage = max(0, emissions - cap)
Fine = overage × $268/tCO2e

Boston BERDO 2.0

Similar structure; penalty rate = $234/tCO2e

Cambridge BEUDO

Mirrors BERDO; penalty rate = $234/tCO2e

9.3 DC BEPS (ENERGY STAR Score Target)

Target score = 71
Fine = sqft × $10 × (gap / target_score)
Cap = $7.5M
Important Constraint

Buildings without ENERGY STAR score cannot be fined under this modeled pathway and are excluded from the DC fine calculation.

9.4 Energize Denver (EUI Target)

Target EUI (office example): 48.3
Overage kBtu = max(0, (EUI - target) × sqft)
Fine = overage_kBtu × $0.30

9.5 Seattle BEPS (Emissions Intensity)

Target GHGI = 0.0023 tCO2e/sqft (commercial)
Cap = sqft × target
Fine = (emissions over cap) × penalty_rate

9.6 St. Louis BEPS (Daily Fine Style)

If EUI > 72: Fine = $500/day × 15 days = $7,500
Otherwise: $0

9.7 San Francisco EBEPO

Explicitly treated as reporting-only (no fine avoidance calculated).

10) Total Annual OpEx Avoidance

10.1 Definition

Total Annual OpEx Avoidance is strictly the sum of:

  • Annual HVAC utility savings
  • Year-1 BPS fine avoidance
total_annual_opex_avoidance = odcv_hvac_savings_annual_usd + bps_fine_avoided_yr1_usd

10.2 Why Valuation is NOT Included

Valuation increase is a downstream capitalization of OpEx avoided; it is not itself an annual operating expense.

11) Valuation Impact Methodology (Income Capitalization)

11.1 Core Concept

If a building avoids $1 in annual operating expense, NOI increases by $1; value increase is NOI uplift divided by cap rate.

11.2 Valuation Uplift Formula

val_odcv_impact_usd = savings_opex_avoided_annual_usd / (cap_rate / 100)

11.3 Cap Rates by Building Type

Building TypeMedian Cap RateValue Multiplier
Office7.50%13.33×
Hotel8.00%12.50×
Retail Store6.25%16.00×
Multifamily5.50%18.18×

11.4 Estimating Current Value

When true valuation is unknown, a "back-of-envelope" estimator is used:

Estimated Gross Income = Total Energy Cost / 0.12
Estimated NOI = Estimated Gross Income × 0.60
val_current_usd = Estimated NOI / (cap_rate / 100)

Then:

val_post_odcv_usd = val_current_usd + val_odcv_impact_usd

11.5 Example

Valuation Impact Example
  • Utility savings: $300k/year
  • Fine avoidance: $800k/year
  • Total OpEx avoided: $1.1M/year
  • At 7% cap rate → ~$15.7M valuation impact

12) Estimated Post-ODCV ENERGY STAR Score

12.1 What an ENERGY STAR Score Is

ENERGY STAR is based on an efficiency ratio:

Efficiency Ratio = Actual Source EUI / Predicted Source EUI

Lower ratio → higher score; mapping uses a gamma distribution per building type.

12.2 How Prospector Estimates Post-ODCV Score

  1. Weighted HVAC fraction: hvac_total_pct = Σ(hvac_pct × energy) / total_energy
  2. Total energy reduction: total_energy_reduction = odcv_pct × hvac_total_pct
  3. New EUI: new_eui = current_eui × (1 - total_energy_reduction)
  4. Convert score ↔ ratio using gamma inverse CDF/CDF with building-type parameters

12.3 Example

A worked example shows score rising from 65 → 73 after ODCV-driven EUI reduction.

Limitations

This is an approximation; actual EPA score calculations rely on proprietary tables and source-energy normalization; site EUI is used as a proxy (estimated 5-10% error).

13) Portfolio Identification Methodology

Nationwide Prospector supports account-based prospecting by defining a portfolio organization as an owner/manager/tenant name that appears across at least 3 separate building rows.

13.1 Normalization Rules (Name Cleaning)

Organization names are normalized by:

  • Lowercasing
  • Stripping punctuation
  • Removing corporate suffixes (LLC, Inc, LP, etc.)
  • Collapsing whitespace
  • Filtering generic/common single-word entities (e.g., "Church", "School")

13.2 Matching & Rollup

  • Organization columns searched: owner / manager / tenant
  • Portfolio threshold: ≥ 3 buildings
  • Output includes a portfolio org label and building counts for targeting workflows

14) Data Quality Controls & Edge-Case Handling

14.1 City Parsing and BPS City Assignment

City is extracted from the address using deterministic parsing and is measured to be highly accurate (reported ~99.996%).

14.2 Emissions from Raw Energy

BPS modeling explicitly computes emissions from raw energy kBtu (not precomputed emissions) to match the original LL97 calculator style.

14.3 Known "Back-Calculated Energy" Records

A documented data-quality patch: 249 records had energy back-calculated from cost using median rates.

14.4 Module-Specific Limitations

  • Cost module: no TOU, tiering, DR credits; rate/multiplier assumptions
  • ODCV: no real occupancy feeds; automation is a proxy; ranges are conservative; validate for proposals
  • BPS: only year-1 caps, office/commercial assumptions, DC needs ENERGY STAR score
  • Valuation: cap rates are market dependent; BPS programs change

15) End-to-End Formula Chain (Single Building)

This is the "audit trail" to reproduce Prospector outputs:

  1. Normalize energy — Convert fuel quantities to kBtu; compute total kBtu and EUI
  2. Compute annual utility costs — Electricity (energy + demand, load-factor peak estimate, multipliers); gas/steam/oil conversions and costs
  3. Estimate HVAC fuel shares — CBECS-based with adjustments and special cases
  4. Compute total HVAC cost — Sum fuel_cost × hvac_pct
  5. Compute ODCV savings % — Floor + (Opportunity × Automation × Range) × modifiers; clamp to type bounds
  6. Compute ODCV annual HVAC savings (USD) — hvac_cost_total_annual × odcv_pct
  7. Compute emissions baseline + post-ODCV — Use city-specific factors; reduce fuels by hvac_pct × odcv_pct
  8. Compute year-1 BPS fines (if applicable) — Apply city's law-type formula; compute avoided fine
  9. Total annual OpEx avoided — utility savings + year-1 fine avoidance
  10. Valuation uplift — OpEx avoided ÷ (cap_rate/100)
  11. Optional: post-ODCV ENERGY STAR score estimate — Weighted HVAC %, EUI reduction, gamma mapping
